Graph 3: Average Crime Rates for Each Hour of the Day

What Time of the Day is When Crime Rate is the Highest?

Crime Rate Vs Time

In the line graph we observe that crime rate through the day does follow a trend. Crime tends to drop dramatically after midnight, and would slowly climb back up at 5am. We then see a sharp increase in crime rate from 11am to 12pm. The highet rate of crime occurs at 6pm, and after slowly drops until the next day morning.

My interest for this question came from wanting to understand when would be the safest time for activities in the city. I removed all the crime reports that were under the non-criminal category, to gather data of only actual crime. Using a line graph was the best option for this graph, because we can observe the change in crime rate with time. From looking at the line graph, it was expected that crime would be low from midnight to the early morning, and would be increasing throughout the day. The sharp increase of crime from 11am to noon did surprise me. The most shocking part of this data was that crime rate peaked at 6pm. Originally I had expected crime to be highest from 7pm - 10pm, since that is when it is dark, but people are still awake. Looking back at my line graph there might be some correlation with the high crime rates and the time that crime rates spike. 12pm may have a crime rate spike because people get off for lunch or have a half work day. At 6pm most people are getting off from work. It seems that times when the city has the most active people crime rate is the highest. There might be a correlation between the number of active people and crime rate.
